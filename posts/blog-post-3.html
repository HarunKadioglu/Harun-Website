Image Processing-Enabled Validation For Robotic Test Automation: A Case Study on POS Devices

October 26, 2024 Robotic Test Automation

Software test automation is a process requiring distinct technical expertise, aimed at making tests faster and more sustainable compared to manual testing. Automated tests enable software testing to be conducted reliably and repeatably, free from human error, without compromising the effectiveness and quality of manual testing. Automation tests are faster and more cost-effective compared to manual testing, as tests can be run without human intervention, allowing test specialists to focus on other tasks.

The most prominent limitation of manual testing is time and cost factors. Manual tests take longer than automated tests, leading to increased costs. Additionally, there may be issues with repeatability; manual tests may not be applied the same way each time, reducing consistency and reliability. Being prone to human error is also a significant disadvantage; factors like lack of attention or fatigue can affect the accuracy of test results. Therefore, test automation has become an essential requirement for improving the effectiveness and accuracy of software testing.

Testing of connected devices is crucial to ensure their secure and compatible operation. These tests enhance the reliability and interoperability of connected systems. Furthermore, they guarantee the performance and security of connected devices. Particularly, testing POS devices that perform financial transactions is of great importance to ensure their secure and accurate operation. Manual tests are typically conducted by software test engineers, examining the devices' functions, data security, and compatibility in detail. This process carries risks of high workload and human error.

The Roboclick project, developed by Virgosol under the KOSGEB R&D, Product Development, and Innovation Support Program, addresses these challenges. The system is designed not only to test the basic functions of POS devices but also to evaluate their operational processes integrated with various platforms such as web, desktop, and mobile applications.

The robotic arm-based system has demonstrated its ability to more effectively assess the performance, reliability, and debugging processes of POS devices. The findings indicate that robotic automation not only accelerates test processes but also has the potential to significantly enhance the accuracy and consistency of test results.

Key advantages of the system include:

  • Increased speed and efficiency in testing processes
  • Enhanced accuracy and consistency in test results
  • Reduced human error in testing procedures
  • Capability to handle complex, multi-stage test scenarios
  • Integration with various platforms (web, desktop, mobile)

In conclusion, this robotic test automation system represents a significant advancement in POS device testing, offering improved efficiency, reliability, and comprehensive testing capabilities. The success of the Roboclick project demonstrates the potential of robotic automation in revolutionizing hardware and software testing processes.
